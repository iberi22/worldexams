### Question

## Question 5 (Medium Variant - Grammar)

**ID:** `CO-ING-11-reading-001-v5`
**Difficulty:** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê (3/5 - Medium)
**CEFR Level:** B1

### Question

Choose the correct sentence:

### Options

- [ ] A) If the government will protect the forests, biodiversity will survive.
- [x] B) If the government protects the forests, biodiversity will survive.
- [ ] C) If the government protected the forests, biodiversity will survive.
- [ ] D) If the government protect the forests, biodiversity will survive.

### Explanation

**Why B is correct:**

This is a **First Conditional** sentence (real possibility in the future):
- **If + Present Simple, ... will + base verb**
- Structure: "If the government **protects** (present), biodiversity **will survive** (future)"

**Why the others are wrong:**
- **A)** "will" is incorrect in the if-clause of first conditional.
- **C)** Mixes second conditional (protected) with first conditional (will survive).
- **D)** "protect" should be "protects" (third person singular).

**Competency evaluated:** Grammar - conditional sentences

### Question

Read the text again and consider the author's purpose. Which of the following best describes the author's main intention?

### Options

- [ ] A) To entertain readers with interesting facts about Colombian wildlife.
- [ ] B) To criticize the Colombian government for not doing enough to protect nature.
- [ ] C) To convince tourists to visit Colombian national parks.
- [x] D) To inform readers about Colombia's biodiversity and argue for its protection.

### Explanation

**Why D is correct:**

The text has **two main purposes**:

   - Links environment to economy
   - Says "more action is needed"
   - Lists required actions (cooperation, laws, community)

The combination of **inform + argue** best describes the text's purpose.

**Why the others are wrong:**
- **A) Entertainment:** The text is serious, not entertaining.
- **B) Criticism:** While problems are mentioned, the focus is broader than criticizing the government.
- **C) Tourism:** This is mentioned briefly but is not the main purpose.

**Competency evaluated:** Critical reading - identifying author's purpose
