# Question
What was unique about the Apple I compared to other computers of its era?

# Options
- [ ] A) It was the first computer with a graphical interface
- [x] B) It came with a fully assembled circuit board
- [ ] C) It included a built-in monitor
- [ ] D) It had color graphics capabilities

# Explanation
The Apple I was unique because it came with a fully assembled circuit board, which was rare at the time when most computers were sold as kits. However, users still needed to add their own case, power supply, keyboard, and display. About 200 units were produced.
- A) The GUI came much later with the Macintosh in 1984
- C) The Apple I did not include a monitor; users had to connect their own
- D) The Apple I only had monochrome text output
